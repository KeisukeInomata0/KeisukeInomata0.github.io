Keisuke Inomata

I am a postdoc at Johns Hopkins University, working on the intersection between Cosmology and high-energy physics.

I am particularly interested in cosmological perturbations as a probe of the early Universe, such as

  • gravitational waves induced by scalar perturbations
  • one-loop correction to scalar power spectrum
  • inflation models for primordial black holes
  • polarization and distortion of cosmic microwave background
